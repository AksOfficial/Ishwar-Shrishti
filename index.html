Current Challenges in Society

Modern society faces an imbalance in its relationship with nature, health, and traditional values. Ishwar Srishti was founded to restore harmony through practical initiatives in cow protection, natural healing, and sustainable agriculture.

Cow Protection

Challenge: Indigenous cow breeds are rapidly declining due to industrial farming.

Our Work: We currently protect and care for 45 indigenous cows at Nandini Gaushala, ensuring medical treatment, pure feed, and natural living conditions.

Healthcare

Challenge: Chronic diseases are increasing due to sedentary lifestyles and chemical-based medicine.

Our Work: We operate a 40-bed registered Naturopathy Hospital offering Panchakarma, Yoga, and holistic therapies.

Agriculture

Challenge: Chemical fertilizers and pesticides are harming soil and human health.

Our Work: Ishwar Srishti practices 100% organic, cow-based farming on nine acres with over 600 trees and 25 plant species.

Ishwar Srishti addresses these issues through a unified approach that connects humans, animals, and the environment in mutual well-being.

About the Project

Ishwar Srishti was founded in 2018 on 9 acres of land in Dinarpura, Sikar (Rajasthan). It is a registered Naturopathy Hospital (E34206(M)) that integrates traditional cow protection, organic agriculture, and naturopathy healing under one initiative.

Nandini Gaushala

Established March 2019 ‚Ä¢ Currently home to 45 indigenous cows (Tharparkar, Rathi, Sahiwal). Produces 16 Panchgavya-based medicines such as Gau Ghrit and Gau Mutra Ark.

Naturopathy Hospital

Operational since November 2018 with 40 beds. Offers Panchakarma, Physiotherapy, Hydrotherapy, Yoga, and natural treatments.

Organic Agriculture

Spread over 9 acres with 600+ trees and 25+ species. 100% chemical-free, cow-based farming model promoting sustainability.

Future Vision

Develop a Ved Vidyapeetham, expand the cow shelter to 100 cows, and establish a Panchgavya Research Laboratory.
